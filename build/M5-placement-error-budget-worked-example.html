Worked Example / Module 5 / CEC-03 / The Placement Error Budget

Every source of error gets one shared budget.

Perception, calibration, estimation, and mechanism each contribute error. This is the contract that binds them: their combined 3-sigma spread must fit inside the gripper's capture tolerance - or the fruit falls outside the jaws. Here it is, worked end to end, then made live.

How the budget is built

Each stage delivers a pose with uncertainty, not a bare point.
Σy  =  J Σx JT\Sigma_y \;=\; J\,\Sigma_x\,J^{\mathsf{T}}Σy​=JΣx​JT

For each stage y=f(x)y = f(x)y=f(x), the covariance transforms through the stage Jacobian JJJ. Chaining the stages accumulates the Jacobians into one fruit-pose covariance in the robot frame. Along the sensitive placement axis, that covariance reduces to a set of independent one-dimensional contributors σi\sigma_iσi​.

Consequence

The pose is delivered with a covariance, not as a bare point (SR-F-08) - uncertainty is a first-class output of perception.

EI-05 - Attack the Dominant Error Source

In a root-sum-square budget, the largest term dominates the total - find it and reduce it first. Halving the dominant depth term buys roughly eight times the improvement of halving a small calibration term, for comparable effort. This is precisely why the architecture stages the fruit close (depth error grows as Z2Z^{2}Z2) instead of chasing calibration to the third decimal. Budget first, find the dominant contributor, spend effort there - then repeat until no single term dominates.

Engineering takeaway / what to do next

Where the next effort belongs

  • -> The budget passes, but with only 3.3 mm of margin - not comfortable. It is worth improving before the pilot, where real-world terms tend to grow.
  • -> Improving calibration by another millimetre moves 3σplace3\sigma_{\mathrm{place}}3σplace​ by well under a millimetre. That effort is almost wasted here.
  • -> Improving stereo depth accuracy - or reducing the working distance so depth error (∼Z2\sim Z^{2}∼Z2) shrinks - produces by far the largest improvement per unit effort.
  • -> Decision: the next engineering effort belongs in perception geometry - the sensor and the staging distance - not in calibration. Re-run the budget after, and re-check which term dominates next.
