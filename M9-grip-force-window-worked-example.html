Worked Example / Module 9 / CEC-02 / The Grip-Force Window

Hold it tight enough not to slip, gentle enough not to bruise.

A watermelon has a window of acceptable grip force. Too little and it slips during the lift and swing; too much and the rind bruises - a harvested melon that earns nothing. This is that window, worked from pressure to force, and the two independent bounds that keep every grasp inside it.

How the window is built

The gripper is a fluid actuator: pressure becomes force through the piston area. We follow Module 9 Sec. 7 exactly, sizing the actuator to the window (Doc B Sec. 5.3) and then closing the two bounds that guarantee the grasp stays inside it.

A fluid actuator is intrinsically compliant - and compliance widens the window.

One bound is computed, one is physical. A software fault can defeat the clip; it cannot defeat the mechanical relief, which vents pressure at physics speed with no detection loop at all (EI-09).

Consequence

No single failure can bruise the fruit past the limit. This is the machine's central safety guarantee - and it is a mechanical part, not a line of code.

Engineering Insight EI-09 / let the physics regulate

The guarantee is a mechanical part

EI-09 - Let the Physics Regulate

Where a physical mechanism can enforce a safety bound, prefer it to a software check. The mechanical relief caps grip force below the bruise threshold at physics speed - no sensing, no computation, no latency, and no software fault that can disable it. The AI setpoint is clipped in software (EI-07) as the first line; the relief is the independent second line that holds even if the first fails. Two bounds, one computed and one physical, are what let an opaque learned policy be trusted on a fragile crop - the whole thesis of fluid-powered physical AI.

Engineering takeaway / what to do next

Where the next effort belongs

  • -> The window is wide ([250,800] N[250,800]\ \mathrm{N}[250,800] N) because the grip is compliant. Do not spend effort making the force controller more precise than the window demands - the compliance already absorbs the error.
  • -> The bruise ceiling depends on contact compliance and pad geometry, not on the force sensor. The highest-leverage improvement is a softer, better-conforming pad - it widens the window on the dangerous side.
  • -> Verify the mechanical relief physically, on the bench, across wear - it is the guarantee, and it must still cap force after a season (feeds the M14 durability test and the Doc E safety case).
  • -> Decision: the next effort belongs in gripper mechanics and the relief - the physical bounds - not in tightening the force loop. The real bruise threshold is a hardware measurement (Doc C Sec. 5 / Doc G R3 rung), and it is the number to go get.
