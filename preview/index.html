Robotic Harvesting Systems · Short Course

Turn a camera frame into a grasped watermelon — in the field, with no cloud.

A practical, engineering-first course on the autonomous harvesting of heavy ground fruit. Seven modules follow the robot's own data path — perceive, decide, act, integrate, deploy — anchored to one fully specified machine you can drive in the browser below.

The curriculum

Seven modules on a signal-to-action spine

Each module exists because the previous one created a need for it. Two carry the live simulation inline where it directly illustrates the engineering.

MODULE 1

The Problem & The System

Why heavy-fruit harvest resists mechanization, the five subsystems, and the signal-to-action path.

◷ 3 hours · live demoOpen →
MODULE 2

Machine Vision & Perception

Detection, stereo depth, 3D localization, and the occlusion gate — finding a green melon in a green canopy.

◷ 5 hoursOpen →
MODULE 3

Edge Computing, ML & AI

On-robot inference under a no-cloud constraint: latency/power budgets, model optimization, learned grasping.

◷ 5 hoursOpen →
MODULE 4

Manipulation & Control

Parallel-mechanism kinematics, workspace and singularities, DOF reduction, and the grasp/place cycle.

◷ 5 hours · live demoOpen →
MODULE 5

Integration & System Design

Hardware and software architecture, measurable specs, the BOM, and the integration hazards that emerge.

◷ 4 hoursOpen →
MODULE 6

Testing, Safety & Deployment

Staged testing, safety interlocks, the labor/ethics debate, and real farm deployment.

◷ 3 hoursOpen →
MODULE 7

Capstone Project

Design and defend an original harvesting subsystem — perception, manipulation, edge, or a whole-system concept for a new crop.
